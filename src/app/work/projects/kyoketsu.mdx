*Kyoketsu is a momentum ninja runner where every jump, slide, and dash flows into a faster, smoother rhythm. So, master your movement, perfect your path and prove you're a worthy ninja!*

## Overview

Kyoketsu is a first-person parkour game prototype centered around the design of smooth, momentum-based, speedrunning-focused gameplay. Players learn how movement mechanics such as dashing, sliding, wallrunning, and grappling can interact with each other to achieve mastery over the levels and beat their personal scores. 

Aside from the level progression and score system, another way Kyoketsu was created with speedrunners in mind is designing base and alternate control schemes for both mouse and keyboard and controller players.

# Features

## Parkour Movement

Smooth parkour movement is a core part of the game. Our top priority was making it functional and intuitive, fixing issues and eliminating bugs so it behaves exactly the way players expect. 

### Dash 
- Tap to dash forward
- Can be used only once in the air
- Can kill dummies
- Charge resets on touching ground, wallrunning, or grappling

### Slide
- Hold to crouch
- Hold while moving to slide
- Can kill dummies if going fast enough
- Gradually slows to a stop

### Wallrun
- Starts when hitting Wallrun game object while falling
- End on pressing jump, or no longer on wall
- Boost jump with increased distance
- Camera tilt

### Grapple
- Shoots rope with ring to Grapple anchor point
- Pulls player towards point
- Cancels if reached point, landed, or line of sight obstructed

## Controls

As a fast-flowing first-person game it's important for the player to be able to easily and naturally move around the level, so the basic controls for both look and movement must be intuitive and ergonomic enough for the player to make quick, responsive inputs.

However, the capacity to do so can be limited by some control schemes. Even this slight limitation makes the movement just a little bit unwieldy and reduces the skill expression of the flow-state style of parkour, so we have provided multiple control schemes to remove that limitation, for players who want to make the trade-off of easier controls for smoother gameplay.

- **Default layouts** are more conventional and beginner-friendly, but some parkour move inputs are positioned in a way that the player is physically able use them while simultaneously operating basic movement
- **Alternative layouts** take a little bit of time to get used to as they're not as conventional, but the placement of the action inputs lets you execute any parkour move without taking your hands off the basic movement controls

## Gameplay Features

Starting a level requires a button press, giving the player a moment to prepare before the timer begins. Players can quick-restart a level to immediately try again if a run goes wrong. Resuming the game triggers a short countdown, giving the player time to get ready before control resumes.

We use two sets of data arrays to manage levels. LevelData is static and holds everything needed to define and set up a level - its name, star rating times, thumbnail image, display name, and the level asset itself. LevelSave is dynamic and stores the player's progress, keeping track of and saving things like their fastest times and unlocked levels.
